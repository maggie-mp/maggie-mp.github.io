Evolving the U-GO Design System to improve stakeholder relationships and accessibility



Role & Timeline


UX/UI Designer and Accessibility Expert
Nov 2025 - Feb 2026 (3 months)

Team


Experience Designers, Developers, QA, Site Systems

Skills


Moderated Interviews, Information Architecture, Stakeholder Management, re-designing Design System

As an Experience Designer, I

  • Led design efforts for rebuilding the U-GO Design System optimising efficiency.
  • Conducted 8 internal moderated interviews with experience designers, delivery lead, retail platform manager, site system analysis, developer, and QA.
  • Pioneered Accessibility automated and manual testing in our Design process using Stark (Figma Plug-in) and manual testing documentation.
  • Collaborated closely with developers and QA testers to understand the complexities of delivering and deployments of the OPT system with external stakeholders like Invenco.
  • Established design review processes to improve handoff between experience designers and developers/QA testers.

Overview:


Z Energy’s Outdoor Payment Terminals are a self-servicing device mounted directly at a fuel pump to allow customers to pay quickly without having to go inside. They’re built for efficacy for customers who want to pump, pay then leave, and reduces pressure on forecourt staff to keep up with demand. Z deploys over 1,600+ payment terminals over two weeks. External stakeholder Invecno, typically give Z Energy Prompt Sets, which include identification.

Problem

Z Energy had set up an OPT Design System hosted on Figma. Despite it being established, the Design System was difficult to navigate, missing key artifacts for designers and developers, underutilising Figma atomic components capabilities, not audited for accessibility, and lacking centralising documentation for all stakeholders.

This made experience designers risk having inconsistent designs for hand-off, developers not having key artefacts to integrated slowing their development, and under-developed user flows with unclear interaction points the user flows and no documentation of error paths, for quality assurance testers.
